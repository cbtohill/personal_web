Welcome!

My name is Clár-Bríd (CB) Tohill and I am a PhD student at the University of Nottingham under the supervision of Dr Steven Bamford and Professor Christopher Conselice. My research focuses on galaxy morphology and evolution with machine learning. My current work is focused on applying unsupervised machine learning techniques to understand and classify the morphology of high redshift galaxies. I am currently working as a support astronomer for the INT at the Isaac Newton Group of telescopes on La Palma.

Outreach

Outreach is a very important aspect of my career. I believe that education should be available to everyone and so one of my main aims is to encourage young people to achieve their potential. I am a manager of the University of Nottingham's Inflatable planetarium which we bring to different primary schools and deliver sessions about the night sky and galaxy classification! During the pandemic I, along with the other managers of the Inflativerse spent time designing different sessions that could be delivered virtually to maintain this contact with schools. We developed sessions about galaxy classification, constellations, learning about what astronomers do and how to navigate the North sky!
